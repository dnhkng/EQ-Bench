March 20th

Those accursed children. They think me a monster, a cruel and heartless witch. But I am not. I am a survivor. A victim of circumstance. They followed me, their eyes wide with wonder, as I led them to my kitchen. And then, the games began. I played along, pretending to be the wicked witch, just to see their reactions. To see if they would truly trust me, truly let their guard down. But they were too smart, too quick. They saw through my ruse, and they fled.

April 10th

I've been thinking, and I've come to a realization. I am not the monster they think me to be. I am a just and fair witch. I am a victim of circumstance, and I will not be judged by those who do not understand.

I've been watching them, the children. Watching them grow, watching them learn. And I've come to realize that they are not so different from me. They are lost, alone, and searching for a place to call their own. Just like me.

"I'll never come back here," Hansel spat, his eyes blazing with hatred. "You're a monster, a witch. You'll never harm anyone else."

Ah, but that's where he's wrong. I am not a monster. I am a survivor. And I will do whatever it takes to survive.

May 20th

I've been thinking about my past, about the fire that destroyed my village. I've been thinking about the people I lost, the lives I destroyed. And I've come to a realization. I am not responsible for the fire. I am not the one who burned it down.

But who is? The question has haunted me for so long, and I think I know the answer. The children. They, with their matches and their careless laughter. They, who think me a monster, but are themselves capable of such destruction.

I will not forget. I will not forgive. And I will make sure they pay for what they've done.

July 10th

I've been watching them, and I've seen the way they look at each other. The way they protect each other, the way they love each other. And I've come to realize that I am not alone. I have my own family, my own tribe.

The children may think me a monster, but I am not. I am a witch, a survivor, a victim. And I will do whatever it takes to protect myself, to protect my family.
