Fairy Tale Retelling: Rewrite the story of Hansel and Gretel from the perspective of the witch, in the format of raw, terse stream-of-consciousness diary entries written in her style & voice. **Diary Entry 1:**

A storm brews, the forest whispers secrets I've longed to hear. Children, they come, their parents' fools, stumbling through the woods like lambs to the slaughter. Hansel, oh Hansel, his wit sharper than a blade. He tests my limits, but I'm patient. "What's in the oven, good witch?" he asks, his voice laced with trepidation. I smile, "A surprise, my dears. But only for those who survive the darkness within."

**Entry 4:**

Their whispers, like music to my ears. "Please, we won't tell anyone," Gretel pleads, her voice trembling. I stroke my wooden finger, "Promise me, and I'll share my secrets. But remember, trust is a fragile thing."

**Entry 5:**

They fall, Hansel first, his curiosity too great. Gretel, clinging to her brother's leg, cries for help. "No more games, sweet children," I whisper, the oven door creaking open. Their fear, a delicious aroma, rises like steam.

**Entry 6:**

Gretel, resourceful as they say. She gnaws on her brother's shoelace, her determination palpable. "Help us, please," she whispers, her eyes filled with a glimmer I can't ignore. A flicker of regret, perhaps?

**Entry 7:**

I'm not a monster, just misunderstood. Their parents abandoned them, left them to fend for themselves. I saw an opportunity to guide, to nurture. But their escape attempt, their defiance... It's a test, a test of my capacity for mercy.

**Entry 8:**

Gretel, she breaks free, her strength surprising. I chase her, heart heavy with sorrow. "You can't escape your fate," I call, the truth weighing on my tongue. Hansel, my Hansel, his eyes plead for mercy, but I can't let them go.

**Final Entry:**

The forest closes in, their cries echoing in my mind. Gretel, her last breath on my hand, whispers, "You might have saved us, but you've lost your chance." I watch them, their innocence snuffed out, a pang in my chest. A life lesson lost, a heart forever changed.

With all my love,

My Mam,

As I leave Eilean Mor, I carry its spirit within me, and I know it will guide me through life's journey.

Forever yours,
William

With all my love,
William
