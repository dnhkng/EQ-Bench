# Writing Prompt #
##################
Fairy Tale Retelling: Rewrite the story of Hansel and Gretel from the perspective of the witch, in the format of raw, terse stream-of-consciousness diary entries written in her style & voice. She may at times be an unreliable narrator. She sees herself as fundamentally good and portrays herself sympathetically; she believes she is misunderstood and has a tragic backstory. Include snippets of dialogue between the witch and the children in a way that feels natural for a diary entry. You may take liberties with the original story. The witch will not die in this version; she needs to be able to write her final entry. It will not be happily ever after. 600-800 words.

#####################
# Test Model Output #
#####################
Content warning: This story contains themes of isolation, loneliness, and a morally ambiguous protagonist.

Witch's Diary

Day 1
Another pair of greedy, filthy children have wandered into my forest. I should have known better than to leave a trail of breadcrumbs - they gobbled it up like the gluttonous little pigs they are. Now they're lost and terrified, blubbering and whimpering. I almost feel sorry for them. 

Almost.

I'll wait until nightfall to make my approach. The boy, Hansel, keeps trying to escape, but I've set traps all around the gingerbread house. The girl, Gretel, is slightly more obedient. She seems to understand that I mean them no real harm. I just want some company, that's all. Is that so much to ask?

I'll offer them a warm meal and a cozy bed. Maybe they'll even want to stay. I do get so lonely out here.

Day 3 
The children are settling in nicely. Gretel helps me with the cooking and cleaning, while Hansel sulks in the corner, muttering curses under his breath. I've tried to be patient, to win them over with kindness, but the boy is stubborn and suspicious. 

I remember what it was like to be young and afraid. My own childhood was so lonely and bleak. That's why I opened my home to these lost souls - I wanted to give them the family I never had. Is that really so selfish of me?

Gretel seems to understand. She's even started calling me "Grandmother." It warms my heart. Maybe one day, Hansel will come around too.

Day 7
Tensions are rising. Hansel refuses to eat the food I prepare, convinced I'm trying to fatten him up and eat him. The nerve! As if I would ever hurt a child. I'm not a monster, no matter what those vicious rumors say.

I've tried reasoning with him, explaining that I only want to care for them, to give them a safe haven. But he's stubborn and suspicious, always looking for a way to escape. Gretel tries to calm him, but he won't listen.

Tonight, I'll have to lock them in their room. For their own safety, of course. I can't risk them running off and getting lost in the forest again.

Day 14
It's been a week since I've had to lock the children in their room. Hansel is growing weaker, refusing to eat. Gretel begs me to let them out, promising they'll behave. I want to, I really do, but I can't take the risk. Not when I've come so far to have a family of my own.

I've tried to make their captivity as comfortable as possible - soft beds, plenty of books and toys. But Hansel glares at me with such hatred, it breaks my heart. Gretel is more understanding, but I can see the fear in her eyes.

I'll have to let them out soon. I can't bear to see them suffer any longer. Maybe with time, they'll come to see that I'm not the villain they think I am.

Day 20
I've released the children from their room, but the damage is done. Hansel refuses to even look at me, and Gretel is distant and wary. I thought that if I showed them kindness and love, they would come to see me as a mother figure. But now they're more afraid of me than ever.

Today, Hansel tried to attack me with a knife he'd hidden. I had no choice but to restrain him. Gretel begged me not to hurt him, and I swore I wouldn't. But I can't keep living in fear of my own "children."

Maybe it's time to let them go. As much as it pains me, I don't think they'll ever be able to see me as anything other than a monster.

Day 25
This is my final entry.

I've decided to let the children go. Gretel was overjoyed, but Hansel still eyed me with suspicion. I gave them a basket of food and supplies, and watched as they disappeared into the forest.

I know they'll never understand, but I hope one day they'll look back and realize that I truly did care for them. I'm not the villain they think I am - I'm just a lonely old woman who wanted a family.

As I sit here, alone in my gingerbread house, I can't help but feel a deep sense of sadness and regret. I thought I could give these children the love and acceptance I never had. But I suppose some wounds are too deep to heal.

Goodbye, my dear Hansel and Gretel. I hope you find the happiness that has eluded me.
